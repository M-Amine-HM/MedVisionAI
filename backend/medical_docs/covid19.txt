COVID-19 (Coronavirus Disease 2019)
Overview:
COVID-19 is caused by SARS-CoV-2, a novel coronavirus first identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019.
It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and aerosols from infected individuals.

Radiological Findings:
Bilateral ground-glass opacities (GGOs) with peripheral and lower-lobe predominance are the hallmark
CT finding. Crazy-paving pattern and consolidation develop in moderate-to-severe disease. Chest X-ray
may appear normal in early or mild cases but shows bilateral infiltrates in moderate disease.

Symptoms:
- Fever and chills (80% of cases)
- Dry cough (60-70%)
- Fatigue and malaise
- Loss of smell (anosmia) and taste (ageusia) — highly specific
- Shortness of breath and dyspnea in moderate-severe disease
- Headache, myalgia, sore throat
- Gastrointestinal symptoms (diarrhea, nausea) in ~20%

Severity Classification:
- Mild: Symptoms without hypoxia. SpO2 ≥ 95%.
- Moderate: Clinical or radiological pneumonia. SpO2 90-94%.
- Severe: SpO2 < 90%, respiratory rate > 30/min, or >50% lung involvement.
- Critical: ARDS, septic shock, multi-organ failure.

Treatment Protocols:
Mild Disease (outpatient):
- Supportive care: rest, hydration, antipyretics (paracetamol)
- Nirmatrelvir/ritonavir (Paxlovid) within 5 days of symptom onset for high-risk patients
- Isolation for minimum 5 days

Moderate Disease (hospitalized):
- Supplemental oxygen to maintain SpO2 ≥ 94%
- Remdesivir (200 mg IV loading, 100 mg daily for 5 days)
- Dexamethasone 6 mg/day for patients requiring oxygen

Severe/Critical Disease (ICU):
- High-flow nasal oxygen or mechanical ventilation
- Dexamethasone 6 mg IV daily for 10 days
- Tocilizumab (IL-6 inhibitor) for hyperinflammatory state
- Prone positioning for ARDS
- Anticoagulation (enoxaparin) for VTE prophylaxis

Prevention:
- mRNA vaccines (Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna): >90% efficacy against severe disease
- Adenoviral vector vaccines (AstraZeneca, J&J)
- N95/FFP2 masks, hand hygiene, ventilation
- Avoid crowded indoor spaces

Complications:
Long COVID: Persistent symptoms (fatigue, brain fog, dyspnea) > 12 weeks post-infection
Thromboembolic events: PE, DVT, stroke
Myocarditis, pericarditis (rare, more common post-vaccination in young males)
Secondary bacterial or fungal infections (aspergillosis)

Next Steps for Patients:
1. Confirm diagnosis with RT-PCR or rapid antigen test
2. Isolate immediately from household contacts
3. Monitor SpO2 with pulse oximeter
4. Seek emergency care if SpO2 < 94%, severe dyspnea, confusion
5. Notify public health authorities (notifiable disease)
6. Contact trace and inform close contacts
