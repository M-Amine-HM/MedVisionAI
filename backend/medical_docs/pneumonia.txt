Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)
Overview:
Pneumonia is an acute infection of the pulmonary parenchyma causing inflammation and fluid/pus
accumulation in the alveoli. It can be bacterial, viral, fungal, or atypical in origin.

Common Causative Organisms:
Bacterial (most common):
- Streptococcus pneumoniae (most common bacterial cause, ~30%)
- Haemophilus influenzae
- Staphylococcus aureus (including MRSA)
- Klebsiella pneumoniae (lobar pneumonia, especially alcoholics)
Atypical:
- Mycoplasma pneumoniae (walking pneumonia, younger patients)
- Legionella pneumophila (Legionnaire's disease)
- Chlamydophila pneumoniae
Viral:
- Influenza A/B
- Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV)
- SARS-CoV-2

Radiological Findings:
- Lobar/segmental consolidation (air space opacification) — classic bacterial
- Interstitial pattern — atypical, viral
- Air bronchograms within consolidation
- Pleural effusion in severe cases
- Cavitation (Klebsiella, Staphylococcus, anaerobes)

Symptoms:
- Acute onset fever (>38.5°C), rigors
- Productive cough with purulent/rusty sputum
- Pleuritic chest pain (sharp, worsens with breathing)
- Dyspnea and tachypnea
- Fatigue, malaise, myalgia
- Confusion in elderly (atypical presentation)

Severity Scoring — CURB-65:
Score 1 point each: Confusion, Urea >7mmol/L, RR ≥30/min, BP (systolic <90 or diastolic ≤60), Age ≥65
- 0-1: Low severity → outpatient
- 2: Moderate → hospital admission
- 3-5: High severity → ICU consideration

Treatment:
Outpatient (CURB-65 0-1):
- Amoxicillin 500mg-1g PO TID × 5-7 days (first-line, non-penicillin-allergic)
- Azithromycin 500mg day 1, 250mg days 2-5 (atypical coverage or penicillin allergy)
- Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 5 days (alternative)

Inpatient, Non-ICU (CURB-65 2-3):
- Beta-lactam (amoxicillin-clavulanate or ceftriaxone 1-2g IV) + macrolide
- OR respiratory fluoroquinolone (levofloxacin 750mg, moxifloxacin 400mg)

ICU / Severe (CURB-65 4-5):
- Beta-lactam + azithromycin OR beta-lactam + fluoroquinolone
- Add anti-MRSA coverage (vancomycin, linezolid) if MRSA risk factors
- Supportive: oxygen, IV fluids, vasopressors if septic shock

Supportive Care:
- Antipyretics (paracetamol, ibuprofen)
- Adequate hydration (oral or IV)
- Incentive spirometry
- DVT prophylaxis (hospitalized patients)
- Smoking cessation counseling

Monitoring:
- SpO2 monitoring (target ≥ 94%)
- Serial blood cultures before antibiotics (ICU patients)
- CRP, procalcitonin for antibiotic stewardship
- Follow-up chest X-ray in 6 weeks to confirm resolution (rule out malignancy)

Prevention:
- Pneumococcal vaccine (PCV20 or PPSV23) — all adults ≥65, immunocompromised
- Annual influenza vaccine
- Smoking cessation
- Good hand hygiene
